Notebook: `notebooks/01_eda.ipynb` (generated by `notebooks/build_eda.py`). Figures in
`reports/figures/`. Metric: **Balanced Accuracy** (hard labels).

## Dataset
- **train** 577,347 ├Ś 12, **test** 247,435 ├Ś 11. Columns:
  `id, alpha, delta, u, g, r, i, z, redshift, spectral_type, galaxy_population, class`.
- **No missing values, no sentinel codes** in either split (clean synthetic data) ŌåÆ the planned
  sentinel/NaN handling is unnecessary. Magnitude mins are physical (~12ŌĆō13), not `-9999`.
- Train and test numeric distributions overlap closely (no obvious drift) ŌĆö local CV should track LB.

## Target (imbalanced)
| class | count | share |
|-------|-------|-------|
| GALAXY | 377,480 | 65.4% |
| QSO    | 117,143 | 20.3% |
| STAR   |  82,724 | 14.3% |

Because the metric is **balanced accuracy**, the minority classes (STAR, QSO) count as much as GALAXY.
Plain-accuracy-optimal models will over-predict GALAXY ŌåÆ we use `class_weight="balanced"` during
training and a **per-class probability multiplier search on OOF** to set the decision rule.

## Most predictive signals
- **`redshift` is the dominant separator** (median by class): STAR Ōēł 0.056, GALAXY Ōēł 0.482,
  QSO Ōēł 1.799. STAR sits near 0 (p99 Ōēł 0.24); QSO is high (p25 Ōēł 1.13). Overlap remains in the
  GALAXYŌåöQSO mid-range, so it is strong but not perfectly separating. `log1p(redshift)` makes the
  separation visually cleaner and is added as a feature.
- **`spectral_type` is highly informative** (P(class | type)):
  - `M`   ŌåÆ 95.0% GALAXY
  - `O/B` ŌåÆ 71.1% QSO
  - `A/F` ŌåÆ 50.4% QSO / 29.8% STAR
  - `G/K` ŌåÆ 56.8% GALAXY / 24.0% STAR
- **`galaxy_population`**: `Red_Sequence` ŌåÆ 90.3% GALAXY; `Blue_Cloud` is mixed (42% QSO / 35% GALAXY).
- **Photometric magnitudes** `u,g,r,i,z` are highly correlated with each other; **color indices**
  (`u-g, g-r, r-i, i-z`) carry the discriminative shape and separate classes better than raw mags.

## Modeling implications
1. Keep both categoricals ŌĆö native categorical support in LightGBM/CatBoost; one-hot for the NN.
2. Engineer color indices + `log1p(redshift)` (done in `src/features.py`).
3. Drop nothing for leakage reasons ŌĆö there are no ID/observation leakage columns here (`id` aside).
4. Train with balanced class weights; **tune the decision rule on OOF for balanced accuracy** rather
   than using raw argmax.
5. Expect strong scores given redshift + spectral_type; the competition will be won on the GALAXYŌåöQSO
   and STARŌåöGALAXY boundaries where signals overlap.
